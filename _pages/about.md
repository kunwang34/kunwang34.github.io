I am a PhD candidate in computational biology with training in probability theory and mathematical statistics. My research develops stochastic dynamical models and statistical inference methods for lineage-resolved single-cell data, with the goal of reconstructing developmental histories and understanding how cell fate decisions emerge from molecular dynamics and ancestry.

I work on probabilistic frameworks that integrate lineage tracing with transcriptomic and multimodal measurements. Rather than treating cells as independent terminal observations, I model gene expression and cellular state transitions as stochastic processes evolving along lineage trees. This perspective makes it possible to infer latent developmental histories, quantify uncertainty, and connect molecular dynamics with tissue growth and tumor evolution.

My recent work includes [PhyloVelo](https://github.com/kunwang34/PhyloVelo), a phylogeny-guided transcriptomic velocity method published in *Nature Biotechnology*, and scPhyloX, a phylodynamic inference framework for stem cell differentiation and tumor evolution published in *Cell Systems*. I also contributed to work on mitochondrial lineage tracing in *Genome Biology* and led a review on computational approaches for multimodal lineage tracing accepted in *Nature Reviews Genetics*.

Research interests
======

- Stochastic dynamical modeling of gene expression along single-cell phylogenies
- Statistical inference and variational methods for lineage-resolved single-cell data
- Cell fate decision processes, developmental dynamics, and tumor evolution
- Endogenous and engineered lineage tracing, including mitochondrial DNA variants and CRISPR-based lineage records

I am based in Shenzhen and Xiamen, working with Prof. Da Zhou and Dr. Zheng Hu.
